# ShiftProof — Product

## Product vision

Every shift in a hospitality venue depends on the same routines being done well: opening the kitchen safely, logging fridge temperatures, cleaning to standard, closing the till correctly. Today that work is invisible — it happens or it doesn't, and owners only find out when something goes wrong.

ShiftProof makes shift routines **visible and provable**. Staff complete routines on their phone as they work, capturing proof along the way. Managers and owners get a trustworthy, time-stamped record and are alerted the moment something is off. The vision is a venue where standards are consistent across every shift, every day, without the owner having to be present.

## Target customer profile

**Primary:** independent and small-group hospitality operators.

- Restaurants and cafés (1–10 locations)
- Boutique hotels and guesthouses
- Bars and food-led venues with recurring compliance and cleaning routines

**Buyer:** owner or operations manager who is accountable for standards and compliance but cannot be on-site for every shift.

**Users:** floor and kitchen staff who run the routines, and shift managers who supervise them.

These teams are too small for heavyweight enterprise operations software, but too serious about standards (and increasingly, food-safety records) to rely on paper.

## Main problems solved

1. **No proof routines were actually done.** Paper checklists get ticked in bulk at the end of a shift, or lost entirely.
2. **Exceptions are discovered too late.** A fridge running warm, a missing delivery, a safety issue — surfaced days later instead of in the moment.
3. **No follow-through.** Problems get noticed but not tracked to resolution.
4. **No visibility across shifts or locations.** Owners can't compare how different shifts or venues are performing.
5. **Compliance anxiety.** When an inspector or auditor asks for records, there's nothing structured to show.

## Value proposition

**ShiftProof turns shift routines into a trustworthy, provable record — with exceptions caught in the moment and followed through to resolution.**

- For **owners**: confidence that standards hold on every shift, and evidence when it's needed.
- For **managers**: a live view of what's done, what's outstanding, and what needs follow-up.
- For **staff**: a fast, phone-friendly way to do the routine and move on.

## Key workflows

1. **Set up routines.** An owner or manager defines routines (e.g. "Opening", "Closing", "Weekly deep clean") for a location, each with an ordered list of tasks.
2. **Run a shift routine.** A staff member starts a routine run, works through the tasks, and captures proof per task: a photo, a value (e.g. a temperature reading), a checkbox, and/or a comment.
3. **Raise exceptions.** When a task can't be completed to standard, the staff member records an exception with detail and optional photo.
4. **Follow up.** A manager reviews exceptions, assigns/records follow-up actions, and marks them resolved.
5. **Review.** Managers and owners review completed runs and open exceptions per location and over time. (Richer reporting comes later.)

## Why now

- Smartphones are universal on the floor — every staff member already carries the capture device.
- Food-safety and compliance expectations keep rising, and paper records are increasingly unacceptable.
- Small operators are under staffing and margin pressure, and can't afford standards slipping when the owner isn't there.
- Modern serverless platforms (Supabase, Railway) make it viable to ship this affordably to small venues.

## Positioning

ShiftProof is the **lightweight proof-of-work layer for hospitality shifts** — not a full operations suite, not an HR/scheduling tool, not a POS.

- **Against paper and spreadsheets:** structured, time-stamped, with real proof and exception follow-through.
- **Against enterprise ops platforms:** dramatically simpler, phone-first, and priced and scoped for small independents.
- **Against generic checklist apps:** purpose-built around hospitality shift routines, proof capture, and exception management.

The wedge is the single, sharp loop: **routine → run → proof → exception → follow-up.** Everything else is deferred until that loop is loved.
